The Pokémon and e-commerce worlds each carry their own dense vocabulary — and when you're selling cards online, you'll run into both at once. Whether you're deciphering a listing description, evaluating a card's condition, or understanding a marketplace payout report, fluency in this shared language will save you time, prevent costly mistakes, and help you communicate confidently with buyers and fellow sellers.

## Card Types & Conditions

<Accordion title="TCG / PTCG">
  **TCG** stands for *Trading Card Game* — the physical card game format. **PTCG** is shorthand for *Pokémon Trading Card Game* specifically. You'll see these abbreviations used everywhere from marketplace category labels to Discord community names. When someone refers to "the TCG market," they mean the broader ecosystem of buying, selling, and playing physical Pokémon cards.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="NM — Near Mint">
  **NM (Near Mint)** is the highest condition grade for raw (ungraded) cards. An NM card has no visible wear, no scratches on the holo surface, sharp corners, and clean edges. Most buyers expect singles listed without a condition note to be NM. Always check both the front and back of a card before assigning this grade.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="LP — Lightly Played">
  **LP (Lightly Played)** cards show very minor wear — slight edge whitening or one or two faint scratches — but remain in excellent overall shape. LP cards are still highly desirable and typically sell for 10–20% below NM market price.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="MP — Moderately Played">
  **MP (Moderately Played)** describes a card with noticeable but not severe wear: moderate edge whitening, light creasing, or surface scuffs. MP cards are common in bulk lots and typically sell at a significant discount to NM.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="HP — Heavily Played">
  **HP (Heavily Played)** cards have obvious wear — deep creases, heavy edge whitening, bends, or ink loss. HP cards still have value in bulk or for casual collectors, but command much lower prices and can be harder to move as singles.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Damaged">
  **Damaged** is the lowest condition tier. A damaged card may have tears, water damage, large creases, missing pieces, or writing on it. These cards are generally only useful as placeholders or for craft purposes, and will sell for very little individually.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Holo">
  A **Holo** (holographic) card features a shiny, reflective foil pattern on the card's illustration or background. The classic holo pattern appears as a starburst or cosmos effect in the art window. Holos are distinct from non-holo rares and typically command a premium.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Reverse Holo">
  A **Reverse Holo** is a card where the *entire card background* (outside the illustration window) is holographic, rather than just the art. Every booster pack contains one reverse holo card. Non-holo rares, uncommons, and even commons can appear as reverse holos.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Full Art">
  A **Full Art** card features artwork that extends across the entire card face, eliminating the standard border and text box. Full Arts are typically Ultra Rares and are highly sought after by collectors for their visual impact.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Alt Art (Alternative Art)">
  **Alt Art** cards are alternate-illustration versions of existing cards, featuring unique artwork distinct from the base version. Introduced prominently in the Sword & Shield era, Alt Arts are among the most collectible and valuable cards in modern sets.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Secret Rare">
  A **Secret Rare** is any card with a collector number exceeding the official set total (e.g., card 236/235). Secret Rares include Rainbow Rares, Gold Cards, Full Arts, and Alt Arts. They have lower pull rates than standard rares and are typically the most valuable cards in a set.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="1st Edition">
  **1st Edition** cards were printed in the very first production run of a set. They are marked with a "1" stamp on the card's left side. First Edition cards from Base Set and early Wizards of the Coast (WOTC) sets are among the most valuable Pokémon cards in existence.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Shadowless">
  **Shadowless** cards are from a short print run between 1st Edition and Unlimited Base Set printings. They lack the drop shadow found on the right side of the illustration box in Unlimited printings. Shadowless Base Set cards are rare and valuable, though less so than 1st Edition.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Unlimited">
  **Unlimited** refers to cards printed after the 1st Edition run with no print limit. Most vintage WOTC cards you encounter will be Unlimited. They include the characteristic drop shadow on the art box and the absence of an edition stamp.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="EX, GX, V, VMAX, ex (Era Abbreviations)">
  These suffixes indicate the card era and mechanic type:

  - **EX** — introduced in the EX Series (Ruby & Sapphire era, 2003) and again in the XY era
  - **GX** — Sun & Moon era cards with powerful one-time GX attacks
  - **V** — Sword & Shield era; the base form of the new V mechanic
  - **VMAX** — the powered-up evolved form of V cards (Sword & Shield era)
  - **ex** (lowercase) — reintroduced in the Scarlet & Violet era as the current mechanic

  Note that **EX** (uppercase, vintage) and **ex** (lowercase, modern) are different card generations entirely.
</Accordion>

## Grading Terms

<Accordion title="PSA — Professional Sports Authenticator">
  **PSA** is the most recognized and widely trusted third-party grading company in the trading card hobby. Cards graded by PSA are sealed in a hard plastic slab with a grade from 1 (Poor) to 10 (Gem Mint). PSA 10s command the highest premiums in the Pokémon market. Turnaround times and pricing vary by service tier.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="BGS — Beckett Grading Services">
  **BGS** is another top-tier grading company known for its subgrade system, which rates Centering, Corners, Edges, and Surface separately alongside an overall grade. BGS 9.5 (Gem Mint) and 10 (Pristine) Pokémon cards are highly collectible, though BGS holds less market share than PSA in Pokémon specifically.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="CGC — Certified Guaranty Company">
  **CGC** expanded from comic books into trading cards and has grown quickly as a grading option. CGC is known for competitive pricing and reasonable turnaround times. CGC grades on a scale of 1–10 and uses a tamper-evident case. It's an increasingly accepted alternative to PSA for many modern Pokémon cards.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Raw Card">
  A **raw card** is an ungraded card — one that has not been submitted to a third-party grading company and is not encased in a slab. Raw cards are the most common form of card sales on platforms like TCGPlayer and eBay. Condition is seller-assessed, making accurate grading critical for maintaining buyer trust.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Slab / Graded Card">
  A **slab** is a graded card that has been authenticated, graded, and sealed inside a hard plastic case by a grading company (PSA, BGS, CGC, etc.). The slab displays the card's grade, certification number, and set details. Slabbed cards typically sell for a premium over equivalent raw cards, especially at high grades.
</Accordion>

## Product Types

<Accordion title="Booster Pack">
  A **Booster Pack** is the standard sealed foil pack containing a fixed number of cards (typically 10 in modern sets) from a specific set. Each pack includes a random assortment of cards with a guaranteed rare or better. Booster packs are the fundamental unit of sealed product.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Booster Box">
  A **Booster Box** contains a fixed number of booster packs — typically 36 packs for English sets. Booster boxes are the most commonly flipped sealed product and are sold both sealed and opened. Expected value (EV) calculations for boxes are a key skill for sealed product sellers.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Elite Trainer Box (ETB)">
  An **Elite Trainer Box (ETB)** is a premium retail product that includes 8–9 booster packs, card sleeves, energy cards, a player's guide, and accessories. ETBs are popular with collectors and players alike and often appreciate in value when sealed, especially for popular sets.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Sealed Product">
  **Sealed product** refers to any Pokémon product that has not been opened — booster boxes, ETBs, booster packs, collections, tins, and premium sets. Sealed product can be held as an investment (it typically appreciates when discontinued) or opened for singles. Selling sealed product generally requires less product knowledge than singles.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pull Rate">
  A **Pull Rate** is the statistical likelihood of pulling a specific card or card type from a booster pack or box. For example, a pull rate of 1:72 packs means you'd expect to open 72 packs before pulling that card. Understanding pull rates helps you calculate the expected value of sealed product before buying or selling.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Lot">
  A **lot** is a bundle of multiple cards or products sold together as a single listing. Lots are a common way to move bulk inventory — for example, "100 random commons/uncommons" or "10 holo rares from Sun & Moon." Lots reduce listing time and can help you clear low-value inventory efficiently.
</Accordion>

## Selling Terms

<Accordion title="COGS — Cost of Goods Sold">
  **COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)** is the direct cost of acquiring the inventory you sell. For a Pokémon seller, COGS includes the purchase price of cards, booster boxes, or collections. Tracking COGS accurately is essential for calculating profit, filing taxes, and understanding your business's financial health.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Market Price / TCGPlayer Market Price">
  **Market Price** refers to the average price at which a card has recently sold — not the asking price. **TCGPlayer Market Price** specifically aggregates recent sales data from TCGPlayer's marketplace to give a real-time benchmark. Always price against market price (based on actual sales) rather than listed prices, which can be inflated.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Buy It Now (BIN)">
  **Buy It Now (BIN)** is a fixed-price listing format on eBay where buyers can purchase immediately at the stated price without bidding. BIN listings are the most common format for card singles and provide price certainty for both buyer and seller. Most experienced sellers prefer BIN over auction for predictable, repeatable results.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Best Offer">
  **Best Offer** is an eBay feature you can enable on BIN listings, allowing buyers to submit a lower price for your consideration. You can accept, decline, or counter any offer. Best Offer listings attract more buyer engagement and can help move slower inventory, though you should set an auto-decline threshold to avoid lowball offers.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Auction">
  An **Auction** listing on eBay starts at a minimum bid and rises as buyers compete, closing after a set duration (typically 1, 3, 5, 7, or 10 days). Auctions work well for rare or high-demand cards where competitive bidding can drive prices above market value, but they introduce income unpredictability and risk selling below market on low-traffic listings.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="INR — Item Not Received">
  **INR (Item Not Received)** is a buyer dispute claim stating that their order never arrived. INR claims are one of the most common seller headaches. Always ship with tracking to protect yourself — without it, you will almost always lose an INR case and be forced to refund. Platforms like eBay typically side with buyers on INR claims unless tracking confirms delivery.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Feedback Score">
  Your **Feedback Score** is a public reputation metric on platforms like eBay, reflecting the number of positive reviews you've received from buyers. A high feedback score builds buyer trust and directly impacts your conversion rate. Negative feedback is hard to remove, so providing fast shipping, accurate descriptions, and responsive communication is essential.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Top Rated Seller">
  **Top Rated Seller** is an eBay designation awarded to sellers who meet specific thresholds for transaction volume, low defect rate, fast shipping, and buyer satisfaction. Top Rated Sellers receive a badge on listings, a discount on final value fees, and higher placement in search results — all of which meaningfully impact sales.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="ROI — Return on Investment">
  **ROI (Return on Investment)** measures how much profit you earn relative to what you spent. It's calculated as: `(Profit ÷ Cost) × 100 = ROI%`. For example, buying a card for $10 and selling it for $15 (after fees) yields a $5 profit and a 50% ROI. Tracking ROI per purchase helps you identify which sourcing channels and product types are most profitable.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Managed Payments">
  **Managed Payments** is eBay's in-house payment processing system, which replaced PayPal as eBay's payment method. Under Managed Payments, eBay collects funds from buyers and deposits payouts directly into your linked bank account. Fees are consolidated into a single final value fee. Understanding Managed Payments is essential for reconciling your eBay earnings accurately.
</Accordion>
