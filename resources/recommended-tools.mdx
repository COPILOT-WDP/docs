The right tools save you hours every week and dramatically increase the accuracy of your pricing, listings, and financial records. You don't need to spend a lot to get started — the majority of tools in this guide have free tiers that are more than sufficient when you're building your first hundred sales. As your volume grows, a few strategic paid upgrades will multiply your output without multiplying your effort.

<Note>
  Most tools listed here offer free tiers that are fully sufficient for sellers just getting started. Don't feel pressured to pay for anything until your volume justifies the expense.
</Note>

## Pricing Research

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="TCGPlayer Market Price" icon="tag" href="https://www.tcgplayer.com">
    The gold standard for pricing Pokémon singles. TCGPlayer aggregates real-time sales data across thousands of listings to display a **Market Price** — the average of recent completed sales. Always price your cards against Market Price, not the lowest listed price. Free to use; no account required to view pricing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="PriceCharting.com" icon="chart-line" href="https://www.pricecharting.com">
    PriceCharting tracks historical price trends for Pokémon cards, sealed product, and video games. Use it to spot long-term appreciation trends on sealed product you're considering holding, or to quickly check graded card values. The free tier covers most use cases; a paid subscription unlocks bulk CSV exports.
  </Card>
  <Card title="eBay Sold Listings" icon="gavel" href="https://www.ebay.com/sh/research">
    eBay's own sold listings filter is one of the most powerful free pricing tools available. Search any card, apply the **Sold Items** filter, and you'll see exactly what real buyers paid in recent weeks. This is especially valuable for graded cards (PSA/BGS/CGC slabs), sealed product, and vintage items where TCGPlayer data may be thin.
  </Card>
  <Card title="130point.com" icon="magnifying-glass-dollar" href="https://www.130point.com">
    130point is a dedicated eBay sales data aggregator that makes it faster to analyze sold listings for Pokémon cards. It strips away noise and surfaces average sale prices, price trends, and sell-through rates in a cleaner interface than eBay's native search. Free to use and particularly helpful for validating BIN prices before you list.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Listing & Inventory Management

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="TCGPlayer Bulk Tool" icon="layer-group" href="https://www.tcgplayer.com/sellercenter">
    If you're selling singles on TCGPlayer, the Bulk Listing Tool lets you search, price, and list dozens of cards at once without creating individual listings. It also surfaces your cards' current market price alongside your listed price so you can stay competitive. Essential for any TCGPlayer seller processing more than a handful of cards at a time.
  </Card>
  <Card title="eBay Seller Hub" icon="store" href="https://www.ebay.com/sh/landing">
    eBay's free Seller Hub is your command center for managing active listings, tracking orders, monitoring your seller metrics, and pulling sales reports. Use the **Performance** tab to track your defect rate and shipping speed — both factors in your Top Rated Seller status. Completely free and built into every eBay seller account.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Airtable (Free Tier)" icon="table" href="https://www.airtable.com">
    Airtable is a flexible database tool that works beautifully as a Pokémon inventory tracker. Build a base to log every card you own, its purchase price, listed price, platform, and sale status. The free tier supports up to 1,000 records per base, which covers most early-stage sellers. Upgrade to paid only when your catalog outgrows the limit.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Google Sheets" icon="file-spreadsheet" href="https://sheets.google.com">
    Don't underestimate Google Sheets — a well-built spreadsheet can handle inventory tracking, COGS calculation, profit tracking, and sales reporting for free. It's the lowest-friction option for beginners and integrates easily with other Google tools. Dozens of free Pokémon seller templates are available online to get you started quickly.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Shipping

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="PirateShip" icon="ship" href="https://www.pirateship.com">
    PirateShip is a free web app that gives you access to deeply discounted USPS Commercial Pricing rates — the same rates big businesses pay — with no monthly fee and no minimum volume. Print labels, schedule pickups, and track shipments all in one place. For most Pokémon sellers shipping PWE, bubble mailers, and small boxes, PirateShip will be your most-used shipping tool.
  </Card>
  <Card title="eBay Shipping Labels" icon="barcode" href="https://www.ebay.com/sh/landing">
    Purchasing shipping labels directly through eBay Seller Hub gives you discounted USPS and UPS rates, automatically marks your order as shipped with tracking, and keeps everything in one workflow. If you're primarily selling on eBay, buying labels through the platform is the simplest and most integrated option.
  </Card>
  <Card title="PayPal Shipping" icon="credit-card" href="https://www.paypal.com/us/webapps/mpp/shipping">
    PayPal Shipping offers discounted USPS label rates and is useful if you receive payments outside of eBay's Managed Payments system (e.g., direct sales, Facebook Marketplace). It's a solid fallback when you're selling across multiple informal channels and need quick label access.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stamps.com" icon="envelope" href="https://www.stamps.com">
    Stamps.com is a full-featured shipping platform used by higher-volume sellers. It supports USPS, UPS, and FedEx, offers batch label printing, and integrates with platforms like eBay and Shopify. It does carry a monthly subscription fee, so it makes more sense once you're shipping dozens of orders per week.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  **PirateShip is the single best shipping upgrade for new sellers.** Sign up for free and instantly access USPS Commercial Pricing — you'll typically save $0.50–$2.00 per shipment over retail post office rates with zero monthly cost. The savings add up to real money fast.
</Tip>

## Bookkeeping

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Wave (Free)" icon="wave-square" href="https://www.waveapps.com">
    Wave is a completely free accounting platform built for small business owners and freelancers. Use it to track income, expenses, and COGS, connect your bank account for automatic transaction import, and generate profit & loss reports at tax time. For a Pokémon seller just getting started, Wave handles everything you need without any subscription cost.
  </Card>
  <Card title="QuickBooks Self-Employed" icon="calculator" href="https://quickbooks.intuit.com/self-employed/">
    QuickBooks Self-Employed is a paid option (monthly subscription) that automatically categorizes transactions, tracks mileage, estimates quarterly taxes, and integrates directly with TurboTax. Once your business generates consistent revenue and you're managing sales tax, the automation justifies the cost.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Google Sheets" icon="file-spreadsheet" href="https://sheets.google.com">
    A purpose-built Google Sheets bookkeeping template is a zero-cost, zero-friction way to track your finances when you're starting out. Log every purchase, sale, and expense manually. It's less automated than Wave or QuickBooks but gives you full visibility and costs nothing. Start here, then migrate to dedicated software when volume demands it.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Photography & Visual Presentation

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Canva (Free Tier)" icon="image" href="https://www.canva.com">
    Canva lets you create clean, professional thumbnail graphics and promotional images for social media, storefronts, and marketing. Use the free tier to design consistent branding for your shop banners, sale announcements, and card showcase posts. The drag-and-drop editor requires no design experience.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Lightbox Kit" icon="lightbulb" href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=tabletop+lightbox+kit">
    A simple tabletop lightbox kit (available for under $30 online) provides consistent, shadow-free lighting for card photography. Clean photos with accurate colors reduce returns, improve buyer confidence, and give your listings a professional edge over competitors shooting in poor lighting.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Smartphone Camera" icon="mobile" href="/setup/listing-best-practices">
    You don't need a DSLR. A modern smartphone camera — especially with Portrait or Macro mode — is more than capable of capturing sharp, accurate card photos. The key variables are **lighting** (use your lightbox) and **focus** (tap the card to lock focus before shooting). Consistent angles and backgrounds matter more than camera hardware.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
