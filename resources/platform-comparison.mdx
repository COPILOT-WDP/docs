Choosing the right mix of selling platforms is one of the highest-leverage decisions you'll make as a Pokémon business owner. Each platform attracts a different buyer, charges different fees, and favors different product types — and the combination you choose will directly shape your revenue, margins, and workload. Understanding where each platform excels lets you route the right inventory to the right audience rather than hoping everything sells in one place.

<Note>
  **Recommended starter combo: eBay + TCGPlayer.** eBay gives you broad reach and flexibility for all product types, while TCGPlayer puts your singles in front of dedicated Pokémon buyers who are ready to purchase. Together, they cover the vast majority of what new sellers need to move inventory quickly.
</Note>

## Platform Overview

| Platform | Seller Fee | Audience Size | Best Product Type | Ease of Setup | Payment Speed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **eBay** | ~13% | Very Large | Everything | Medium | Fast |
| **TCGPlayer** | ~12.5% | Large (TCG-focused) | Singles | Easy | Weekly |
| **Whatnot** | ~8% + payment | Medium | Sealed, Live Breaks | Medium | Fast |
| **Etsy** | ~6.5% + listing fee | Medium | Vintage, Art Cards | Easy | Daily |
| **Shopify** | ~2% + payment processing | Self-built | Everything | Hard | Daily |
| **Facebook Marketplace** | Free / Low | Local | Bulk, Lots | Very Easy | Immediate |

## Platform Deep Dives

<Accordion title="eBay — The All-Purpose Powerhouse">
  **Best for:** Singles, sealed product, graded cards, bulk lots, vintage cards — essentially anything Pokémon-related.

  **Pros:**
  - Largest general buyer audience in the world; incredible reach for rare and high-value cards
  - Flexible listing formats: BIN, Best Offer, and Auction
  - Strong seller protections when you ship with tracking and follow platform policies
  - Powerful Seller Hub analytics help you optimize pricing and listing strategy
  - Graded card (PSA/BGS/CGC) buyers actively shop eBay — it's the dominant secondary market for slabs

  **Cons:**
  - Final value fees of approximately 13% (including payment processing) are among the highest of any platform
  - Buyer disputes and INR claims can be stressful for new sellers; eBay often defaults to buyer-friendly resolutions
  - High competition in popular card categories means listings need strong titles, accurate grading, and competitive pricing to stand out
  - Learning the platform fully — promoted listings, fee structures, defect metrics — takes time

  **Fee breakdown:** eBay charges a final value fee (typically 13.25% for trading cards up to $7,500) plus a $0.30 per-order transaction fee. There are no listing fees for your first 250 listings per month.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="TCGPlayer — The Singles Specialist">
  **Best for:** Individual Pokémon card singles (raw and graded), priced at or near market value.

  **Pros:**
  - Purpose-built for TCG singles; buyers arrive already knowing what they want and what it's worth
  - Market Price algorithm makes competitive pricing straightforward — your cards surface when they're priced right
  - Bulk listing tool dramatically reduces time-to-list for high-volume sellers
  - Buyers trust the platform and the condition grading standards, reducing disputes
  - Direct Pro seller tier available for higher-volume operations

  **Cons:**
  - Payouts are weekly, not instant — cash flow can lag behind sales
  - Platform fees (~12.5% including payment processing) are comparable to eBay
  - Less effective for sealed product, graded slabs, or vintage cards with niche demand
  - You are competing on price more directly than on other platforms — thin margin differentiation

  **Fee breakdown:** TCGPlayer charges a seller fee that varies by seller tier (typically 10.25%–12.25%) plus payment processing. Direct sellers receive slightly better rates.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Whatnot — The Live Selling Arena">
  **Best for:** Sealed product, booster box breaks, live pack openings, and building a loyal repeat customer base.

  **Pros:**
  - Live auction format creates excitement and urgency, driving prices above static listing values on popular items
  - Lower base seller fee (~8%) compared to eBay and TCGPlayer
  - Strong community and repeat buyer dynamic — good streamers build loyal followings fast
  - Fast payouts after shows complete
  - Excellent discovery for new sellers willing to invest time in building a show schedule

  **Cons:**
  - Requires on-camera time and real-time audience engagement — not passive selling
  - Building an audience takes consistent effort; early shows may have low viewership
  - Product-to-camera workflow (filming pack openings cleanly) requires equipment and practice
  - Payment processing fees add to the base seller fee

  **Fee breakdown:** Whatnot charges approximately 8% seller fee plus standard payment processing fees.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Etsy — The Vintage & Collector Niche">
  **Best for:** Vintage WOTC-era cards, art cards, Pokémon-themed merchandise, and collector-grade items with aesthetic appeal.

  **Pros:**
  - Lower base seller fee (6.5%) than eBay or TCGPlayer
  - Buyers on Etsy actively search for unique, vintage, and collectible items — ideal positioning for WOTC Base Set, Jungle, Fossil era cards
  - Daily payouts via Etsy Payments
  - Easy-to-use seller dashboard; low technical barrier to setup

  **Cons:**
  - Smaller total audience than eBay; Pokémon-specific buyer traffic is lower
  - $0.20 per listing fee applies (renewed every 4 months or upon sale)
  - Not suited for modern singles or standard sealed product — buyers come for the vintage/art angle
  - Etsy's terms of service require you to accurately represent your products; misrepresenting cards can result in account suspension

  **Fee breakdown:** 6.5% transaction fee + $0.20 listing fee + payment processing (~3% + $0.25).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Shopify — Your Own Storefront">
  **Best for:** Established sellers ready to own their customer relationships, build a brand, and reduce long-term platform dependency.

  **Pros:**
  - Full control over your storefront design, branding, customer data, and pricing
  - Lowest platform transaction fee (~2% on Basic plan, 0% if using Shopify Payments)
  - Ideal for selling direct to repeat customers, running email campaigns, and building subscription boxes or bundles
  - Integrates with eBay, TCGPlayer, and other platforms via third-party apps

  **Cons:**
  - No built-in audience — you must drive all your own traffic through ads, SEO, or social media
  - Monthly subscription fee ($39+/month) regardless of sales volume
  - Significant setup, design, and ongoing maintenance effort compared to marketplace platforms
  - Best results come after you have an existing customer base to migrate; starting from zero is difficult

  **Fee breakdown:** $39+/month subscription + 2% transaction fee (or 0% with Shopify Payments) + payment processing (~2.9% + $0.30).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Facebook Marketplace — The Local & Bulk Mover">
  **Best for:** Bulk lots, local pickups, large collection sales, and low-value inventory not worth paying platform fees on.

  **Pros:**
  - Zero or very low fees (Facebook charges a 5% selling fee for shipped items; local pickup is free)
  - Immediate cash for local sales — no waiting on payouts
  - Great for moving bulk commons, large lots, and binder collections that would be unprofitable after eBay fees
  - Easy to set up; nearly everyone already has a Facebook account

  **Cons:**
  - Limited buyer trust infrastructure — no formal feedback system, no platform-enforced dispute resolution
  - Higher risk of scams, no-shows, and flaky buyers compared to established marketplaces
  - Not suitable for premium singles or graded cards where buyer trust and verified authenticity matter
  - Reach is primarily local unless you enable shipping (which introduces the fee)

  **Fee breakdown:** Free for local in-person sales; 5% fee (minimum $0.40) for shipped Marketplace orders.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Once you're generating consistent monthly revenue and have a growing repeat customer base, launching a Shopify store lets you capture sales without paying 12–13% platform fees on every transaction. Use your existing marketplace presence to drive traffic to your store and retain customers long-term — the margin difference compounds quickly at scale.
</Tip>
