Getting the legal and financial foundation of your business right from day one is far less painful than trying to untangle it later. Many new sellers treat their Pokémon business like a casual hobby for the first year, only to face surprise tax bills, platform reporting forms they weren't expecting, or complications when they try to get a reseller permit retroactively. A few hours of setup early on saves you significant stress — and money — as your business scales.

<Note>
  The information on this page is general educational guidance only and does not constitute legal or tax advice. Tax laws vary by state and country and change frequently. Consult a licensed accountant or tax attorney for advice specific to your situation before making decisions about your business structure or tax obligations.
</Note>

## Business Structure: Sole Proprietor vs. LLC

When you start selling, you're automatically operating as a **sole proprietor** — there's no paperwork required. Your business income is reported on your personal tax return (Schedule C in the US), and you're personally liable for any business debts or legal claims.

Forming a **Limited Liability Company (LLC)** creates a legal separation between you and your business. If someone sues your business or you have outstanding business debt, your personal assets (car, home, savings) are generally protected. An LLC also adds credibility when opening business bank accounts and applying for reseller permits.

Most early-stage Pokémon sellers start as sole proprietors and transition to an LLC once revenue becomes consistent — typically when annual gross sales cross $10,000–$20,000. LLC formation costs between $50 and $500 depending on your state, with most states requiring an annual renewal fee.

## Sales Tax Obligations for Online Sellers

Sales tax rules for online sellers are complex and have changed significantly since the 2018 *South Dakota v. Wayfair* Supreme Court decision, which allowed states to require sellers to collect sales tax even without a physical presence in the state.

Key concepts to understand:

- **Nexus** — The connection between your business and a state that triggers a sales tax obligation. You have nexus in your home state automatically. You may establish economic nexus in other states once you exceed their sales threshold (commonly $100,000 in sales or 200 transactions in a calendar year).
- **Marketplace Facilitator Laws** — Platforms like eBay and TCGPlayer are classified as "marketplace facilitators" in most states, meaning they collect and remit sales tax on your behalf for transactions made through their platform. This significantly reduces your direct obligation for those sales.
- **Personal Store Sales** — If you sell through your own website (Shopify, etc.), you are responsible for collecting and remitting sales tax in states where you have nexus. Use a tool like TaxJar or Avalara to automate this.

## Income Tax: Profit vs. Cost of Goods Sold

You pay income tax on your **profit**, not your gross revenue. Your profit equals your total sales revenue minus your **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)** and other deductible business expenses.

**COGS** includes everything you paid to acquire your inventory — the purchase price of cards, booster boxes, collection lots, and grading fees. Other deductible expenses typically include:

- Platform fees (eBay final value fees, TCGPlayer fees)
- Shipping supplies and postage
- Grading fees and shipping to/from graders
- Business portion of internet and phone bills
- Accounting software subscriptions

Tracking COGS accurately is the single most impactful thing you can do to reduce your tax liability. Every dollar of COGS is a dollar of revenue that is not taxed.

## Record Keeping: What to Track

Maintain a record for every transaction. At minimum, track:

| Field | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Purchase date & price** | Establishes your cost basis for COGS |
| **Item description** | Ties the purchase to a specific card or lot |
| **Sale date & price** | Determines revenue for the tax year |
| **Platform fees** | Reduces taxable profit |
| **Shipping cost (paid)** | Deductible business expense |
| **Shipping received** | Part of gross revenue if charged to buyer |
| **Grading fees** | Can be added to COGS or expensed separately |

Keep digital copies of all receipts and invoices. Cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox) is free and makes records accessible for years without risk of physical loss.

## Platform 1099-K Forms

Starting in recent tax years, the IRS lowered the threshold for platforms to issue **Form 1099-K** to sellers. eBay, TCGPlayer, PayPal, and other platforms are required to report your gross sales to the IRS and send you a copy when you meet the reporting threshold.

Important points:
- A 1099-K reports **gross revenue**, not profit — it does not account for your COGS or expenses
- Receiving a 1099-K does not mean you owe tax on that full amount; it means the IRS knows you had that revenue
- You must still file accurately, reporting your COGS and expenses to arrive at your actual taxable income
- Make sure your address and tax ID on each platform are current so forms reach you

## Reseller Permits and Sales Tax Exemption

A **reseller permit** (also called a resale certificate or seller's permit) is issued by your state and allows you to purchase inventory for resale without paying sales tax at the point of purchase. Since you'll collect and remit sales tax when you sell the item, paying it when you buy it would create double taxation.

When buying wholesale Pokémon product from a distributor or at a card show, presenting your reseller permit exempts you from sales tax — which directly improves your margins. Most states issue permits for free or a nominal fee through the state's Department of Revenue website.

## Getting Set Up: Your Legal Foundation Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register Your Business">
    Decide on sole proprietor or LLC. If forming an LLC, file Articles of Organization with your state's Secretary of State office. Obtain an **EIN (Employer Identification Number)** from the IRS for free at irs.gov — you'll need this to open a business bank account and apply for permits, even if you have no employees.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get Your Reseller Permit">
    Apply for a reseller or seller's permit through your state's Department of Revenue (most states have an online application). This permit lets you buy inventory tax-free from wholesalers and distributors. Keep copies to present when making wholesale purchases.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a Business Bank Account">
    Open a dedicated business checking account separate from your personal finances. This is the single most important step for clean record-keeping. Commingling personal and business funds makes bookkeeping a nightmare and can jeopardize LLC liability protection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Up Bookkeeping">
    Choose a bookkeeping method before your first sale, not after. A spreadsheet works at the start, but dedicated software makes tax prep far faster. Connect your business bank account to your bookkeeping tool so transactions import automatically. Set aside time weekly to categorize transactions while they're fresh.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Wave** (free) and **QuickBooks Self-Employed** (~$15/month) are both excellent starting points for Pokémon sellers. Wave is fully free and handles income, expenses, and basic invoicing well. QuickBooks Self-Employed automatically estimates your quarterly tax payments based on your profit, which is invaluable for avoiding underpayment penalties as your business grows.
</Tip>
