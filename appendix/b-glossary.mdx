The manual's own handles are marked ▸; terms of the trade are unmarked. Chapter numbers point
to the full treatment.

- **ABLs** ▸: always be learning, always be listing. The manual's closing rule. ([6.3](/chapters/6-3-going-full-time-or-not))
- **Bands, the** ▸: the deliberate adjustments around the 70% anchor. ([2.1](/chapters/2-1-you-make-money-when-you-buy))
- **Bankroll**: money that's allowed to lose; never rent money. ([5.2](/chapters/5-2-bankroll-discipline))
- **Big is just boring, repeated** ▸: scale is systems run consistently, not drama. ([6.1](/chapters/6-1-systems-before-scale))
- **Bulk**: low-value cards traded in volume; cents-per-card math. "The school of the business." ([3.1](/chapters/3-1-the-four-product-classes))
- **Buy the rung you're standing on** ▸: tools and buys sized to your current operation, not your imagined one. ([6.1](/chapters/6-1-systems-before-scale), [5.2](/chapters/5-2-bankroll-discipline))
- **Capital in a box** ▸: sealed product: cash locked in chunky, condition-sensitive units. ([3.1](/chapters/3-1-the-four-product-classes))
- **Case premium**: the price bonus a factory-sealed case carries over loose boxes. ([3.1](/chapters/3-1-the-four-product-classes))
- **Cashflow is oxygen** ▸: margin is how well you breathe; cash flow is whether you're breathing at all. ([2.3](/chapters/2-3-cashflow-is-oxygen))
- **Chaos sorting**: listing bulk unsorted, software-assisted, sorted-on-sale. Opposite: traditional sorting. ([4.3](/chapters/4-3-listing-and-inventory-discipline))
- **Change wheel, the**: an operator's term for how fast you inspect and adapt; too slow misses shifts. ([6.1](/chapters/6-1-systems-before-scale))
- **Churn and burn**: the operators' own phrase: sell fast, rebuy, repeat; velocity over holding. ([2.3](/chapters/2-3-cashflow-is-oxygen))
- **Coin-flip rule, the** ▸: if you're stuck choosing a platform, flip a coin and start selling. ([1.4](/chapters/1-4-validate-before-you-build), [4.2](/chapters/4-2-choosing-where-to-sell))
- **Collector creep** ▸: hobbyist instincts quietly re-entering business decisions. ([1.1](/chapters/1-1-the-merchant-mindset))
- **Comps**: recently *sold* prices, the only real market value. Listings are wishes. ([2.1](/chapters/2-1-you-make-money-when-you-buy))
- **Constraint is the curriculum, the** ▸: a small bankroll forces velocity and teaches faster. ([5.2](/chapters/5-2-bankroll-discipline))
- **Cost stack, the** ▸: every selling cost as a share of gross: fees, tax, shipping, supplies, losses, time. Lean ~8%, typical ~15%. ([2.2](/chapters/2-2-the-real-cost-stack))
- **Deal flow**: the stream of buying opportunities; built by visibility, not found. ([4.1](/chapters/4-1-sourcing-the-ladder))
- **Don't-need list, the** ▸: LLC, scanner, software, polished site, big capital: amplifiers, not prerequisites. ([1.3](/chapters/1-3-the-three-ingredients))
- **Flake tax, the** ▸: the hours lost to no-shows and lowballers on local marketplaces. ([4.2](/chapters/4-2-choosing-where-to-sell))
- **Floor pile, the** ▸: your unlisted backlog. Worth \$0 until listed. ([4.3](/chapters/4-3-listing-and-inventory-discipline))
- **Fully-loaded hour, the** ▸: a venue's true time cost including prep and travel. ([4.2](/chapters/4-2-choosing-where-to-sell))
- **Good-evening test, the** ▸: after 20 orders: can packing night still be a good evening? ([4.4](/chapters/4-4-fulfillment-and-the-customer-promise), [1.4](/chapters/1-4-validate-before-you-build))
- **Guilt test, the** ▸: if selling it would feel like a loss, it's collection, not inventory. ([1.1](/chapters/1-1-the-merchant-mindset))
- **Hobby with extra steps, a** ▸: a card operation whose buy price can't survive its cost stack. ([2.1](/chapters/2-1-you-make-money-when-you-buy))
- **Level four**: a marketplace seller tier (~50 good orders) unlocking storefront fee rates and bulk tools. ([4.2](/chapters/4-2-choosing-where-to-sell))
- **Merchant, not collector** ▸: the identity shift: inventory exists to leave. ([1.1](/chapters/1-1-the-merchant-mindset))
- **Misery is not fuel** ▸: hating your job is not a business plan. ([6.3](/chapters/6-3-going-full-time-or-not))
- **No-leverage rule, the** ▸: inventory is bought with cash; a float you couldn't repay today is debt. ([5.2](/chapters/5-2-bankroll-discipline))
- **One lane, run deep** ▸: as you grow, focus one core lane before adding another. ([3.2](/chapters/3-2-matching-class-to-capital))
- **Platform is a landlord, the** ▸: venue dependence is rented ground; own a way to reach customers. ([5.3](/chapters/5-3-the-risk-map))
- **Priced to sit is priced to lose** ▸: inventory that doesn't turn is stored risk plus dead capital. ([2.1](/chapters/2-1-you-make-money-when-you-buy), [5.3](/chapters/5-3-the-risk-map))
- **Proof-of-work marketing** ▸: content that shows the operation instead of advertising it. ([6.2](/chapters/6-2-content-is-a-moat))
- **PWE**: plain white envelope; the ~\$0.85 bottom packaging tier. ([4.4](/chapters/4-4-fulfillment-and-the-customer-promise))
- **Seed round, your collection is your** ▸: startup capital comes from cards you already own. ([1.3](/chapters/1-3-the-three-ingredients))
- **70% anchor, the** ▸: the default collection offer; the number that survives costs, time, and mistakes. ([2.1](/chapters/2-1-you-make-money-when-you-buy))
- **70/95/5 engine, the** ▸: buy at 70, sell at 95, pay ~5 in fees on a lean stack. ([2.4](/chapters/2-4-selling-is-a-math-problem))
- **Sifter**: bulk-sorting machine. Earns its keep only where bulk is already profitable. ([4.3](/chapters/4-3-listing-and-inventory-discipline), [6.1](/chapters/6-1-systems-before-scale))
- **Slabs**: graded, encapsulated cards; high ticket, capital in a queue while grading. ([3.1](/chapters/3-1-the-four-product-classes))
- **Spread follows the work, the** ▸: buy prices rise with the labor already done: bulk pennies → singles ~70% → liquid sealed ~80% → slabs 85–90%. ([3.1](/chapters/3-1-the-four-product-classes))
- **Stack gets paid first, the** ▸: fees, taxes, shipping and losses come out before you do. ([2.2](/chapters/2-2-the-real-cost-stack))
- **Three ingredients, the** ▸: time, knowledge, inventory; miss one and it costs you in the other two. ([1.3](/chapters/1-3-the-three-ingredients))
- **Three numbers, the** ▸: sales, profit, cash: know all three or you know nothing. ([2.3](/chapters/2-3-cashflow-is-oxygen))
- **Threshold ledger, the** ▸: the documented bars for going full-time: income replaced, 6–12 months runway. ([6.3](/chapters/6-3-going-full-time-or-not))
- **Tuition period, the** ▸: the early months when breaking even while learning is success. ([1.2](/chapters/1-2-what-success-actually-looks-like))
- **20-order gate, the** ▸: no structural spending until ~20 orders are shipped. ([1.4](/chapters/1-4-validate-before-you-build))
- **Two points buy the pipeline** ▸: paying slightly more can purchase deal flow, once velocity is proven. ([2.1](/chapters/2-1-you-make-money-when-you-buy))
- **Velocity is the hedge** ▸: fast turns replace market prediction as your risk defense. ([5.3](/chapters/5-3-the-risk-map))
- **Working capital wearing a costume** ▸: "investment" inventory that's really your bankroll standing still. ([2.3](/chapters/2-3-cashflow-is-oxygen))
