title: "Appendix B — Glossary"
description: "The manual's handles and the trade's terms, one line each."
---

The manual's own handles are marked ▸; terms of the trade are unmarked. Chapter numbers point
to the full treatment.

- **ABLs** ▸ — always be learning, always be listing. The manual's closing rule. (6.3)
- **Bands, the** ▸ — the deliberate adjustments around the 70% anchor. (2.1)
- **Bankroll** — money that's allowed to lose; never rent money. (5.2)
- **Big is just boring, repeated** ▸ — scale is systems run consistently, not drama. (6.1)
- **Bulk** — low-value cards traded in volume; cents-per-card math. "The school of the business." (3.1)
- **Buy the rung you're standing on** ▸ — tools and buys sized to your current operation, not your imagined one. (6.1, 5.2)
- **Capital in a box** ▸ — sealed product: cash locked in chunky, condition-sensitive units. (3.1)
- **Case premium** — the price bonus a factory-sealed case carries over loose boxes. (3.1)
- **Cashflow is oxygen** ▸ — margin is how well you breathe; cash flow is whether you're breathing at all. (2.3)
- **Chaos sorting** — listing bulk unsorted, software-assisted, sorted-on-sale. Opposite: traditional sorting. (4.3)
- **Change wheel, the** — an operator's term for how fast you inspect and adapt; too slow misses shifts. (6.1)
- **Churn and burn** — the operators' own phrase: sell fast, rebuy, repeat; velocity over holding. (2.3)
- **Coin-flip rule, the** ▸ — if you're stuck choosing a platform, flip a coin and start selling. (1.4, 4.2)
- **Collector creep** ▸ — hobbyist instincts quietly re-entering business decisions. (1.1)
- **Comps** — recently *sold* prices, the only real market value. Listings are wishes. (2.1)
- **Constraint is the curriculum, the** ▸ — a small bankroll forces velocity and teaches faster. (5.2)
- **Cost stack, the** ▸ — every selling cost as a share of gross: fees, tax, shipping, supplies, losses, time. Lean ~8%, typical ~15%. (2.2)
- **Deal flow** — the stream of buying opportunities; built by visibility, not found. (4.1)
- **Don't-need list, the** ▸ — LLC, scanner, software, polished site, big capital: amplifiers, not prerequisites. (1.3)
- **Flake tax, the** ▸ — the hours lost to no-shows and lowballers on local marketplaces. (4.2)
- **Floor pile, the** ▸ — your unlisted backlog. Worth $0 until listed. (4.3)
- **Fully-loaded hour, the** ▸ — a venue's true time cost including prep and travel. (4.2)
- **Good-evening test, the** ▸ — after 20 orders: can packing night still be a good evening? (4.4, 1.4)
- **Guilt test, the** ▸ — if selling it would feel like a loss, it's collection, not inventory. (1.1)
- **Hobby with extra steps, a** ▸ — a card operation whose buy price can't survive its cost stack. (2.1)
- **Level four** — a marketplace seller tier (~50 good orders) unlocking storefront fee rates and bulk tools. (4.2)
- **Merchant, not collector** ▸ — the identity shift: inventory exists to leave. (1.1)
- **Misery is not fuel** ▸ — hating your job is not a business plan. (6.3)
- **No-leverage rule, the** ▸ — inventory is bought with cash; a float you couldn't repay today is debt. (5.2)
- **One lane, run deep** ▸ — as you grow, focus one core lane before adding another. (3.2)
- **Platform is a landlord, the** ▸ — venue dependence is rented ground; own a way to reach customers. (5.3)
- **Priced to sit is priced to lose** ▸ — inventory that doesn't turn is stored risk plus dead capital. (2.1, 5.3)
- **Proof-of-work marketing** ▸ — content that shows the operation instead of advertising it. (6.2)
- **PWE** — plain white envelope; the ~$0.85 bottom packaging tier. (4.4)
- **Seed round, your collection is your** ▸ — startup capital comes from cards you already own. (1.3)
- **70% anchor, the** ▸ — the default collection offer; the number that survives costs, time, and mistakes. (2.1)
- **70/95/5 engine, the** ▸ — buy at 70, sell at 95, pay ~5 in fees on a lean stack. (2.4)
- **Sifter** — bulk-sorting machine. Earns its keep only where bulk is already profitable. (4.3, 6.1)
- **Slabs** — graded, encapsulated cards; high ticket, capital in a queue while grading. (3.1)
- **Spread follows the work, the** ▸ — buy prices rise with the labor already done: bulk pennies → singles ~70% → liquid sealed ~80% → slabs 85–90%. (3.1)
- **Stack gets paid first, the** ▸ — fees, taxes, shipping and losses come out before you do. (2.2)
- **Three ingredients, the** ▸ — time, knowledge, inventory; miss one and it costs you in the other two. (1.3)
- **Three numbers, the** ▸ — sales, profit, cash: know all three or you know nothing. (2.3)
- **Threshold ledger, the** ▸ — the documented bars for going full-time: income replaced, 6–12 months runway. (6.3)
- **Tuition period, the** ▸ — the early months when breaking even while learning is success. (1.2)
- **20-order gate, the** ▸ — no structural spending until ~20 orders are shipped. (1.4)
- **Two points buy the pipeline** ▸ — paying slightly more can purchase deal flow — when velocity is proven. (2.1)
- **Velocity is the hedge** ▸ — fast turns replace market prediction as your risk defense. (5.3)
- **Working capital wearing a costume** ▸ — "investment" inventory that's really your bankroll standing still. (2.3)
