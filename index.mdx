Everything in this manual was learned in public.

Operators who built real card businesses — six figures in sales from a basement, evenings only;
a store started with $22,000 of inventory and a lot of nerve; a million-dollar year built one
reinvested flip at a time — documented what worked and what didn't, on camera, with numbers.
This manual mines those hundreds of hours down to the principles, organizes them into a system,
and gives it to you for free.

Free because the knowledge was already free. It was just scattered across a thousand videos.

## The thesis

**Selling Pokemon cards is easy. Building a Pokemon card business is a discipline.**

Anyone can list a card tonight. The difference between a seller and a business is a handful of
principles applied without exception: buy right, turn fast, know your costs, protect your
bankroll, build systems. That's the whole manual in one sentence. The chapters are the details.

## Who this is for

- You have a binder, a few hundred dollars, and a suspicion this could be more than a hobby.
- You're already selling and want the operating system: math, systems, discipline.
- You're deciding whether this business deserves your next two years.

Who it's not for: anyone hunting price predictions or "the next set to buy." This is not an
investing guide. We teach you to run a business that doesn't need a crystal ball.

## How to use it

Read Parts 1 and 2 in order — they rewire how you think about buying, costs, and cash. Read
Part 3 before your first serious purchase. Parts 4 through 6 are stage-based: use them as you
hit sourcing, channel, scaling, and go-full-time decisions. The appendices — quick-reference
tables, glossary, checklists — are the pages you'll come back to. This is a manual, not a
novel; returning is the point.

Every chapter ends with **In practice** — questions to answer before you act. If you can't
answer them, that's the chapter telling you you're not ready yet. That's it doing its job.

## How it was built, honestly

Every substantive claim in this manual traces back to documented operator experience — real
deals, real percentages, real failures, verified against the original sources. Where operators
genuinely disagree, we show you both sides and give you the framework, not a verdict. Opinions
are marked as opinions. Numbers are quoted as the operators stated them, from their time and
their businesses — treat them as evidence, not gospel.

The people whose work made this possible are credited in the Attribution appendix. Watch them.
They're still teaching for free, and they're better at it than any paywall.

Nothing here is for sale. There is no upsell, no cohort, no "advanced tier." If this manual
helps you, pass it on to the next person with a binder and a suspicion.
