Your feedback score is your most valuable asset as an online seller — more than your inventory, your photos, or your prices. A string of five-star reviews signals to every future buyer that you are safe to purchase from, and platforms like eBay reward high-rated sellers with better search visibility and reduced fees. Protecting that score is not just good customer service; it is a core business strategy.

## Response Time and Communication Standards

Speed matters in online selling. Buyers who send a message and hear nothing for 48 hours often open a dispute or leave negative feedback out of anxiety, not malice. Commit to replying within 24 hours every day, including weekends — most of your competition will not, and that alone puts you ahead. Use platform messaging systems (eBay Messages, TCGPlayer's inbox) rather than personal email so all communication is documented.

Keep your tone calm, professional, and solution-oriented even when a buyer is frustrated. A warm, clear response to a complaint defuses the vast majority of situations before they escalate.

<Tip>
  Include a printed thank-you card with every single order. A short handwritten note — "Thanks so much for your order! Enjoy the cards 🎴" — takes ten seconds to write and dramatically increases the likelihood of a positive follow-up review. Buyers who feel appreciated are buyers who leave five stars unprompted.
</Tip>

## Resolving a Dispute

When something goes wrong — and eventually, something will — follow this process to resolve it professionally and protect your feedback score.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Acknowledge the Issue">
    Reply promptly and confirm that you have received and read the buyer's message. Do not be defensive. A simple "I'm so sorry to hear there was a problem with your order — I want to make this right" goes further than any explanation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Apologize Genuinely">
    Even if the issue was caused by a carrier, a packaging failure, or circumstances outside your control, take ownership of the buyer's experience. Buyers do not need you to be perfect — they need to feel heard.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Offer a Clear Solution">
    Present one or two concrete options: a full refund, a replacement shipment, a partial refund, or store credit. Be specific about what you will do and by when. Vague promises create more anxiety. "I will issue a full refund within 24 hours" is far more reassuring than "I'll look into it."
  </Step>

  <Step title="Follow Through Completely">
    Execute exactly what you promised, on the timeline you committed to. If you said you would reship within two days, reship within two days. Following through transforms a bad experience into a trust-building moment.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask for Updated Feedback">
    Once the issue is resolved and the buyer confirms they are satisfied, politely ask if they would be willing to revise their feedback or leave a new review. Most satisfied buyers are happy to help — they just need a gentle reminder. Never pressure or demand; simply invite.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Scenarios

<Accordion title="Item Lost in the Mail (INR — Item Not Received)">
  An INR case opens when a buyer reports their package never arrived. First, check the tracking information and share it with the buyer. If tracking shows delivered, ask the buyer to check with neighbors and their local post office — packages are frequently mis-scanned or left with a neighbor.

  If tracking shows the package is genuinely stuck or lost, file a claim with USPS, UPS, or your carrier and issue a refund or replacement to the buyer promptly — do not wait for the claim to resolve. eBay will side with the buyer in most INR cases regardless, and acting fast protects your feedback. Always ship with tracking; it is your only protection in these situations.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Buyer Claims Wrong Item Received">
  Ask the buyer to send a clear photo of what they received. Review your own records — a photo of the card before shipping, your listing photos, and your packing notes if you keep them. If the error was yours, apologize sincerely and offer an immediate solution: reship the correct item or issue a full refund.

  If you are confident the correct item was shipped, remain professional and present your evidence calmly. Escalate to platform resolution only after attempting a direct solution — most genuine mix-ups are resolved without a formal case.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Negative Feedback Received">
  Read the feedback carefully before responding. If the complaint is valid, acknowledge it publicly with a brief, professional reply that shows future buyers you take issues seriously and resolve them. Never match the buyer's tone if they are upset — your response is a public advertisement for how you handle problems.

  Contact the buyer privately through platform messaging to understand their concern and offer a resolution. Most platforms allow buyers to revise feedback after a dispute is resolved. If the feedback violates platform policies (contains personal information, profanity, or is factually false), you can request platform removal — but use this option sparingly and only when genuinely warranted.

  <Warning>
    Never respond to negative feedback with a retaliatory or defensive public comment. Future buyers read your responses — a petty or aggressive reply to one unhappy customer will cost you far more sales than the original negative feedback ever would.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Return Request">
  Review your stated return policy before responding — consistency matters. If you accept returns, provide a prepaid return label for items that arrived damaged or were misrepresented. For buyer's remorse returns (the buyer simply changed their mind), you may charge return shipping if your policy states it.

  Process refunds promptly once the item is returned and inspected. A smooth, drama-free return experience often results in the buyer leaving positive feedback anyway — they remember how you handled the problem, not the problem itself.
</Accordion>

## Building Positive Feedback Proactively

The best defense against negative feedback is a large bank of positive feedback that makes any single bad review statistically irrelevant. Build it deliberately:

- **Ship fast.** Same-day or next-day shipping is one of the most-cited reasons buyers leave five stars. Set a handling time you can genuinely beat, then beat it consistently.
- **Pack well.** Cards should arrive in the same condition they left. Use penny sleeves, top loaders, team bags, and bubble mailers or boxes appropriate to the order value. Damage in transit is always your problem in the buyer's eyes.
- **Send a follow-up message.** A short message sent three to five days after delivery — "Just checking in to make sure your order arrived safely! Hope you love the cards" — prompts buyers to leave feedback who might otherwise forget.

## eBay Top Rated Seller Status

eBay's Top Rated Seller (TRS) program rewards sellers with better search placement, a trust badge on listings, and up to a 10% final value fee discount. Requirements include: an active account for at least 90 days, 100+ transactions and $1,000+ in sales in the past 12 months, a transaction defect rate below 0.5%, and a late shipment rate below 3%. Maintaining TRS is primarily a matter of shipping on time and resolving disputes before they become formal defects — both well within your control with the habits outlined on this page.
