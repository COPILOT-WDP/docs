Social media is the highest-leverage free marketing channel available to Pokémon sellers today. A single pack-opening video can reach tens of thousands of people who have never heard of your store, and a well-timed post on the right subreddit can move inventory in hours. Unlike paid ads, the audience you build on social platforms compounds — every follower you earn makes the next post perform better.

## Platform Breakdown

Use the tabs below to understand what each platform demands and what it delivers in return.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="TikTok & Reels">
    | | Details |
    |---|---|
    | **Content Type** | Pack openings, card reveals, "is this card worth grading?" videos, storytime content about big pulls or collection milestones |
    | **Posting Frequency** | 3–5 times per week for consistent growth; daily during new set releases |
    | **Best For** | Top-of-funnel discovery — reaching buyers who don't know you yet |

    TikTok's algorithm actively shows your content to non-followers, making it the single best platform for organic reach. Film pack openings vertically, keep videos under 60 seconds initially, and add text overlays so viewers can follow along without sound. Use trending audio when it fits naturally. React genuinely to pulls — authenticity outperforms polish every time on this platform.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Instagram">
    | | Details |
    |---|---|
    | **Content Type** | High-quality card photography, collection showcases, before/after grading posts, Stories polls ("Which would you pull?"), Reels repurposed from TikTok |
    | **Posting Frequency** | 4–5 feed posts per week; Stories daily |
    | **Best For** | Visual brand-building and re-engaging warm followers |

    Instagram rewards aesthetics. Invest in a lightbox or a simple photo setup with a neutral background and good lighting — your cards should look crisp and desirable. Use Stories to run polls, share behind-the-scenes packing clips, and post flash sales. Reels get significantly more reach than static posts, so repurpose your TikTok content here without removing the watermark (use SnapTik or a similar tool first).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="YouTube">
    | | Details |
    |---|---|
    | **Content Type** | Long-form pack openings, buying guides ("Best Cards to Buy in 2025"), collection tours, grading submission vlogs, set reviews |
    | **Posting Frequency** | 1–2 times per week |
    | **Best For** | Building deep trust, SEO-driven discovery, and monetization over time |

    YouTube is a long game — it takes three to six months of consistent posting before most channels see meaningful growth. But the payoff is substantial: YouTube videos rank in Google search, viewers watch for longer and trust you more deeply, and the platform pays ad revenue once you hit 1,000 subscribers. Start with evergreen buying guides and set reviews that answer questions buyers are already searching for.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Reddit">
    | | Details |
    |---|---|
    | **Content Type** | Value checks, trade offers, collection shares, honest questions — never overt self-promotion |
    | **Posting Frequency** | As needed; quality over quantity |
    | **Best For** | Community credibility, peer-to-peer trades, and niche expertise |

    Reddit's Pokémon communities are highly engaged but allergic to spam. Read each subreddit's rules carefully before posting — **r/pkmntcgtrades** has strict formatting rules for trade/sale posts, **r/PokemonCardValue** is for value questions only, and **r/pokemoncardcollectors** rewards genuine collection sharing over selling. Build karma by being genuinely helpful before promoting your store. A single highly-upvoted post can drive hundreds of profile visits.

    <Warning>
      Do not post direct links to your eBay listings, store pages, or social profiles in most Reddit communities — this is treated as spam and will get your posts removed or your account banned. Build reputation first; let your profile link do the selling passively.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Facebook Groups">
    | | Details |
    |---|---|
    | **Content Type** | Buy/sell/trade posts, collection photos, local meetup announcements |
    | **Posting Frequency** | Per group rules; typically 1–2 posts per week per group |
    | **Best For** | Local buyers, bulk movers, and high-trust peer-to-peer sales |

    Facebook Groups remain highly active for Pokémon buying and selling, especially at the local level. Search for groups by city or region for local pickup sales, and join national groups like "Pokémon Card Collectors & Traders" for broader reach. Always read pinned posts and group rules — many groups prohibit direct links to external platforms like eBay.

    <Warning>
      Many Facebook buy/sell groups explicitly ban links to eBay, TCGPlayer, or personal websites. Post prices and photos directly in the group instead of linking out. Violating this rule can get you removed from groups that took time to build standing in.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Whatnot">
    | | Details |
    |---|---|
    | **Content Type** | Live pack openings, live auctions, mystery box reveals, "pick your card" interactive streams |
    | **Posting Frequency** | 1–4 live streams per week once approved as a seller |
    | **Best For** | High-energy sales, impulse buyers, and building a loyal live audience |

    Whatnot combines entertainment and commerce in real time. Buyers bid on cards live, which often drives prices above static listing values for desirable pulls. Apply for seller status through the Whatnot app — approval typically takes one to two weeks. Promote upcoming streams on your TikTok and Instagram 24–48 hours in advance to seed your audience before going live.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Film one strong piece of content — a pack opening, a card reveal, or a collection showcase — and repurpose it across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts in a single editing session. Remove any platform watermarks before cross-posting (use SnapTik for TikTok videos). One shoot, three platforms, three times the reach.
</Tip>

## Build Your Social Presence Consistently

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose 1–2 Platforms">
    Do not spread yourself thin by trying to be everywhere at once. Pick the one or two platforms where your target buyers already spend time — TikTok and Instagram are the highest-return starting point for most Pokémon sellers — and go deep before expanding.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Post Consistently for 30 Days">
    Commit to your posting schedule for a full 30 days without checking analytics obsessively. Algorithms reward consistency, and most accounts do not see meaningful traction until weeks three or four. Think of the first month as paying into a system that will pay you back later.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Engage with Followers">
    Reply to every comment and DM you receive, especially in the first hour after posting — early engagement signals to algorithms that your content is worth amplifying. Ask questions in your captions ("What set are you collecting right now?") to invite responses and build a community feel.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Promote Listings Organically">
    Once you have an engaged audience, weave your listings naturally into your content. Show the card in a video, mention it is in your store, and add a link in your bio. Never lead with "buy this" — lead with entertainment or education, and let the sale follow. Followers who trust you are far more likely to buy than cold traffic from ads.
  </Step>
</Steps>
