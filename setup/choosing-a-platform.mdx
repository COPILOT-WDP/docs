The platform you choose shapes everything about your Pokémon business — your audience size, the fees eating into your margins, and the daily workflow you'll live with. Before you list a single card, spend time understanding where your target buyers actually shop and which platform aligns with what you're selling.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="eBay">
    **Audience**

    eBay is the largest general marketplace in the world, with millions of active collectors and casual buyers searching for Pokémon cards every day. Its broad reach means exposure to both seasoned collectors hunting specific graded cards and parents buying birthday presents.

    **Fees**

    Expect to pay approximately **13%** in combined final value fees (including payment processing). Fee structures vary slightly by category and seller tier, so review your eBay seller dashboard for exact rates.

    **Best For**

    - Graded cards (PSA, BGS, CGC slabs)
    - Factory-sealed booster boxes and ETBs
    - Bulk lots and collections
    - High-value singles where a broad auction format drives competitive bids

      <Card title="Pros" icon="circle-check" href="/resources/platform-comparison">
        - Massive buyer base
        - Auction format creates price discovery

      </Card>
      <Card title="Cons" icon="circle-xmark" href="/resources/platform-comparison">
        - Fees are among the highest
        - High competition on common cards
        - Buyer disputes can favor the buyer
        - Interface can feel dated
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="TCGPlayer">
    **Audience**

    TCGPlayer is the go-to marketplace for trading card game players and collectors. Its audience skews toward knowledgeable buyers who know exactly what card they want and are comparing prices across multiple sellers simultaneously.

    **Fees**

    TCGPlayer charges approximately **12.5%** in seller fees, which includes payment processing. Direct sellers who ship from their own inventory pay lower fees than those using TCGPlayer's fulfillment program.

    **Best For**

    - Individual singles at competitive market prices
    - Complete sets and near-complete sets
    - Bulk commons and uncommons
    - Sellers with consistent, organized inventory

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Pros" icon="circle-check" href="/resources/platform-comparison">
        - Built-in pricing algorithm guides you to market rate
        - Buyers already know TCG values — less education needed
        - Bulk listing tools save time
        - Strong repeat-buyer potential
      </Card>
      <Card title="Cons" icon="circle-xmark" href="/resources/platform-comparison">
        - Race-to-the-bottom pricing on high-supply cards
        - Less effective for graded slabs
        - Requires consistent inventory management
        - Smaller audience than eBay
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Whatnot">
    **Audience**

    Whatnot is a live-streaming marketplace where sellers break packs, showcase collections, and sell cards in real time. The audience is entertainment-driven — buyers come for the experience as much as the cards. Community and personality matter here.

    **Fees**

    Whatnot charges approximately **8%** in seller fees plus payment processing. While fees are lower, success depends heavily on building a live audience, which takes time and consistent streaming effort.

    **Best For**

    - Factory-sealed product and pack breaks
    - Entertainment-focused selling (unboxings, reveals)
    - Sellers who are comfortable on camera
    - Building a loyal community of repeat buyers

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Pros" icon="circle-check" href="/resources/platform-comparison">
        - Lower fees than eBay
        - High engagement drives impulse purchases
        - Loyal community builds repeat revenue
        - Great for moving sealed product fast
      </Card>
      <Card title="Cons" icon="circle-xmark" href="/resources/platform-comparison">
        - Requires time investment in live streaming
        - Revenue is unpredictable early on
        - Less suitable for passive/asynchronous selling
        - Competitive for new streamers to gain traction
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Etsy">
    **Audience**

    Etsy attracts buyers looking for unique, handmade, vintage, or artistic items. Pokémon card sellers who thrive here typically offer something beyond a standard single — think vintage Base Set cards, custom display pieces, art cards, or curated nostalgia bundles.

    **Fees**

    Etsy charges a **6.5% transaction fee** plus a $0.20 listing fee per item and payment processing fees (~3%). Total effective fees generally land around 10–12% depending on your pricing.

    **Best For**

    - Vintage and 1st Edition cards with a nostalgia angle
    - Custom Pokémon card art and altered cards
    - Gift-ready bundles and presentation sets
    - Sellers who want to tell a story around their products

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Pros" icon="circle-check" href="/resources/platform-comparison">
        - Less price competition than eBay or TCGPlayer
        - Buyers expect to pay a premium for uniqueness
        - Strong gifting season traffic
        - Good SEO for niche search terms
      </Card>
      <Card title="Cons" icon="circle-xmark" href="/resources/platform-comparison">
        - Smaller TCG-specific buyer pool
        - Not suited for bulk singles or standard listings
        - Requires strong photography and branding
        - Multiple fee layers add up quickly
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Shopify / Own Store">
    **Audience**

    Running your own Shopify store means you own the customer relationship entirely — but you're also responsible for driving every visitor to your site. Your audience is whoever you build through social media, email marketing, and SEO. This is a long-game strategy.

    **Fees**

    Shopify plans start at **$39/month** with payment processing fees around 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction (or lower with Shopify Payments). There are no per-listing fees, making it cost-effective at high volume.

    **Best For**

    - Established sellers with an existing audience or brand
    - Sellers who want full control over pricing, branding, and customer data
    - Subscription boxes, memberships, or recurring orders
    - Diversifying away from marketplace dependency

    <CardGroup cols={2}>

  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  **Start with eBay + TCGPlayer.** This combination gives you access to both the broad general marketplace (eBay) and the dedicated TCG collector audience (TCGPlayer). Together they cover the vast majority of Pokémon card buyers and let you test pricing, learn the market, and build seller feedback without overcomplicating your workflow.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Resist the urge to list on every platform at once. Managing inventory, responding to messages, and fulfilling orders across five marketplaces simultaneously is a fast path to burnout and mistakes. Master one or two platforms first, then expand once your systems are solid.
</Tip>
