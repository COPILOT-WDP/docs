title: "Pokémon Card Listing Best Practices for Online Sellers"
sidebarTitle: "Listing Tips"
description: "Learn to write compelling titles, shoot great photos, and craft descriptions that turn browsers into buyers for your Pokémon card listings."
---

A great listing is the difference between a sale and a scroll-past. Buyers on eBay and TCGPlayer are comparing dozens of listings at once — your title, thumbnail photo, and first sentence of description need to do the heavy lifting before anyone even clicks. Get these fundamentals right and your conversion rate will climb.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Take Photos">
    Strong photography is your single biggest trust signal. Buyers can't hold the card, so your images do the work of proving condition and authenticity.

    - Shoot in natural daylight or use a lightbox for consistent, shadow-free lighting
    - Use a plain **white or black background** — it keeps the card sharp and the focus clean
    - Capture **multiple angles**: front, back, all four corners, and both edges
    - Photograph any condition issues (scratches, whitening, bends) clearly — hiding flaws leads to disputes
    - Shoot graded slabs at an angle to show the label and all four sides of the case
    - Use the highest resolution your camera or phone allows; buyers zoom in
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write Your Title">
    Your title is what search algorithms and buyers read first. Use every character available (eBay gives you 80) and follow this proven formula:

    ```plaintext
    [Year] [Set Name] [Card Name] [Rarity] [Card Number] [Condition or Grade]

    Example:
    1999 Pokémon Base Set Charizard Holo Rare 4/102 PSA 10 Gem Mint
    ```

    Pack in the keywords collectors actually search for. Include terms like:
    - **holo**, **reverse holo**, **full art**, **secret rare**
    - **shadowless**, **1st edition**, **unlimited**
    - **PSA 10**, **BGS 9.5**, **CGC 10**, **near mint**
    - The exact set name and card number (e.g., 4/102, 025/185)

    Avoid filler words like "wow," "look," or "L@@K" — they waste space and signal amateur listings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write Your Description">
    A thorough description answers every question a buyer might have before they need to ask it. Include all of the following:

    - **Exact card name** as printed on the card
    - **Set name and number** (e.g., Sword & Shield — Brilliant Stars, 020/172)
    - **Condition** using standard descriptors (see below)
    - **What's included** — is it raw or graded? In a sleeve? With a top loader?
    - **Shipping details** — handling time, carrier, whether you ship internationally
    - **Return policy** — be explicit so buyers know what to expect

    **Condition Descriptors:**

    | Grade | Meaning |
    |-------|---------|
    | **NM** (Near Mint) | Virtually no wear; looks pack-fresh |
    | **LP** (Lightly Played) | Minor edge wear or light scratches; still presentable |
    | **MP** (Moderately Played) | Noticeable wear, possible creasing; clearly used |
    | **HP** (Heavily Played) | Significant wear, bends, or heavy scratching |
    | **Damaged** | Tears, water damage, writing, or major structural issues |
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Your Price">
    Before entering a price, check recent sold listings on eBay and the current market price on TCGPlayer. List **slightly above your target sale price** — this gives you room to accept Best Offers or run a small markdown promotion without going below your margin floor.

    Factor in:
    - Platform fees (typically 12–13%)
    - Shipping cost (or your flat-rate shipping policy)
    - Packing materials
    - Your purchase cost or replacement cost
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish and Monitor">
    After publishing, check your listing as a buyer would: search for the card name and see where your listing ranks. If it's buried, revisit your title keywords. Monitor views and watchers in your seller dashboard — high views with no sales often signal a pricing issue, while low views indicate a title or keyword problem.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use eBay's **"Sell Similar"** feature to copy the structure of top-performing completed listings. Find a sold listing for the same card at a strong price, click "Sell Similar," and use it as your starting template. You'll inherit proven keywords and formatting instead of starting from scratch.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  **Never misrepresent card condition.** Overstating a card's grade — even accidentally — is the fastest way to earn negative feedback, lose seller status, and trigger costly return disputes. When in doubt, grade conservatively. A buyer pleasantly surprised by better-than-expected condition leaves a five-star review; a buyer who feels misled leaves a one-star review and opens a case.
</Warning>
