How you pack and ship cards directly impacts buyer satisfaction and return rates. A card that arrives damaged, lost, or bent because of poor packaging will cost you far more in refunds, negative feedback, and replacement cards than the few cents you saved on materials. Build a consistent, professional packing and shipping process from day one and protect both your cards and your seller reputation.

## Payment Methods

Before you can get paid, make sure your payment setup is solid. Most platforms manage this for you, but it's worth understanding what each method means for your cash flow and dispute exposure.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="eBay Managed Payments" icon="credit-card" href="/setup/choosing-a-platform">
    eBay handles all payment processing directly. Funds are deposited to your linked bank account within 1–2 business days after the order is confirmed. You cannot accept PayPal for eBay transactions — Managed Payments is mandatory. Dispute resolution goes through eBay's system.
  </Card>
  <Card title="TCGPlayer Payments" icon="credit-card" href="/setup/choosing-a-platform">
    TCGPlayer similarly manages all payments through its platform. Sellers receive payouts on a schedule based on shipment confirmation. TCGPlayer's buyer protection program handles disputes, which generally move faster than eBay's process.
  </Card>
  <Card title="PayPal" icon="paypal" href="/resources/recommended-tools">
    PayPal is still commonly used for peer-to-peer sales (Discord groups, Facebook groups, local meetups). Use **Goods & Services** — never Friends & Family — to ensure both you and the buyer are protected. Friends & Family transactions offer zero recourse if something goes wrong.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stripe" icon="building-columns" href="/resources/recommended-tools">
    If you operate your own Shopify store, Stripe or Shopify Payments processes your transactions. Stripe is reliable, widely trusted, and deposits funds on a 2-day rolling basis. Enable Shopify Payments if available in your region for the lowest processing fees.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Packing Your Cards

Packing is where most beginner sellers cut corners — and where most damage happens. Follow these standards consistently.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sleeve the Card">
    Place the card face-first into a **penny sleeve** (soft poly sleeve). This protects the surface from scratches caused by contact with the top loader. For higher-value cards, double-sleeve: penny sleeve first, then a perfect-fit sleeve beneath it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Insert into a Top Loader">
    Slide the sleeved card into a **top loader** — a rigid clear plastic holder. Use the correct thickness:
    - Standard cards: 35pt top loader
    - Thicker cards (V, VMAX, ex): 55pt or 75pt top loader

    The card should fit snugly with no wiggle room. Tape the opening with a small piece of painter's tape or a card saver sticker to prevent the card from sliding out.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Seal in a Team Bag">
    Place the top loader inside a **team bag** (a resealable plastic sleeve). This protects against moisture, condensation during transit, and any liquid exposure in the shipping process. Seal it closed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a Cardboard Sandwich">
    Cut two pieces of cardboard slightly larger than the top loader and place one on each side. Secure with rubber bands or tape. This prevents the rigid top loader from being bent, punctured, or damaged if the package is squeezed or dropped.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Place in a Bubble Mailer">
    Slide the cardboard sandwich into a **bubble mailer**. For a single card, a 4"×8" bubble mailer is typically sufficient. For multiple cards or a top loader stack, use a larger padded envelope. Do not use plain paper envelopes — they offer no protection and USPS sorting machines will destroy them.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Affix Label and Ship">
    Print or write your shipping label clearly. Include both the recipient address and your return address. Purchase tracking at checkout — for USPS First Class, tracking is included in the label price. Drop off at a USPS location or schedule a pickup.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Packing Sets and Lots**

For multiple cards or complete sets, stack your top loaders together and wrap the bundle in a sheet of bubble wrap before placing it in a padded envelope or small box. Add a **cardboard stiffener** on each side of the stack. For valuable lots (over $50 total), use a small shipping box instead of a mailer for added rigidity.

**Packing Sealed Product**

Keep sealed booster boxes and ETBs in their original packaging. Wrap in at least one layer of bubble wrap, then ship in a box that's 2–3 inches larger on each side than the product. Fill void space with packing paper or air pillows. Do not use a mailer for sealed product — boxes only.

## Choosing a Shipping Carrier

<Accordion title="USPS First Class (singles and light packages)">
  The most cost-effective option for single cards and small lots. First Class packages under 1 oz cost under $1.00 when purchased online. Includes tracking. Delivery typically takes 2–5 business days. This is the standard for nearly all individual card shipments.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="USPS Priority Mail">
  Use Priority for heavier lots, sets, or sealed product under 70 lbs. Flat Rate envelopes and boxes simplify pricing regardless of weight. Priority includes $100 of insurance automatically and typically delivers in 1–3 business days.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="UPS and FedEx">
  Better for heavy sealed product or high-value shipments where you want carrier-level insurance and more reliable handling. Rates are higher than USPS for small packages but competitive for larger boxes. Both offer declared value coverage up to thousands of dollars.
</Accordion>

## Insurance and Tracking

**Tracking** gives you and your buyer visibility into the shipment and protects you from "item not received" disputes. For any order over **$20**, always purchase tracked shipping. Without tracking, you have no evidence a package was delivered and platforms will typically side with the buyer in a dispute.

**Insurance** adds financial protection if a package is lost or damaged in transit. USPS Priority Mail includes $100 of insurance automatically. For any card valued over **$50**, purchase additional declared value coverage or third-party shipping insurance (services like InsurePost or Shipsurance are widely used by card sellers).

## International Shipping

International orders come with higher risk and higher fees. Customs forms are required for all international packages — fill them out accurately (do not mark packages as "gift" to avoid customs; this is fraud). Delivery times vary widely (1–6+ weeks), and lost international packages are much harder to resolve than domestic ones. Consider restricting international shipping until you're confident in your process, or charge a premium that accounts for the added risk.

<Warning>
  **Never ship a high-value card without insurance and tracking.** A single lost or damaged package on a $200+ card — with no tracking and no insurance — is an unrecoverable loss. The cost of tracked, insured shipping is a fraction of the card's value. Make it non-negotiable for anything over $50, and mandatory for anything over $20.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Buy your packing supplies in **bulk** to significantly reduce your per-shipment cost. A pack of 100 penny sleeves costs roughly the same as 10 from a local store. Top loaders, bubble mailers, and team bags all follow the same logic — buy 100 or 500 at a time from Amazon or a wholesale supplier. Once you're shipping 20+ orders a week, bulk supplies pay for themselves within days.
</Tip>
